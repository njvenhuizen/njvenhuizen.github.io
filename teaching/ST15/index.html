Course Overview

Semantic Theory is a core course of the Master of Science pogramme in Language Science and Technology (LST), taught at the department of Computational Linguistics and Phonetics at Saarland University.

Lecturer: Noortje Venhuizen: noortjev@coli.uni-saarland.de
Place: Building C7.2, seminar room (1.12)
Time: Tue 10-12, Thu 10-12
Starts: 23.04.2015

Prerequisites: Familiarity with first-order predicate logic.

Literature: Recommended reading material for this course:

  • Gamut, Logic, Language, and Meaning, Vol. 2, University of Chicago Press, 1991
  • Kamp/Reyle, From Discourse to Logic, Kluwer, 1993

Exercises and Exam: There will be a written exam at the end of the semester. The grade you get on this exam is the grade for the course.

There will be 9 sets of exercises, spread out over the weeks. Exercises can be done in teams of up to three students. Each team turns in only one copy of their solution, with the names of all the members of the team clearly marked on the solution.

To be admitted to the final exam, you need to have obtained a 'pass' grade for at least 8 out of the 9 exercises (that is, you may skip or fail one exercise sheet).

Schedule

The course takes place on Tuesdays and Thursdays at 10:15-11:45. These sessions will be flexibly used for lectures and others for exercise discussions. Sometimes we will start a session with exercise discussions and then continue with a lecture. So you are encouraged to participate regularly if you don't want to miss anything.
